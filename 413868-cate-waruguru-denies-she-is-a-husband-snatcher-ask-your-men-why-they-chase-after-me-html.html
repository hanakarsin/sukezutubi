The 34-year-old mother is a great example of grass to grace. Waruguru, an orphan, was raised by her grandmother, who later passed away in 2013 before she was sworn in as a Member of the County Assembly (MCA).

Waruguru is currently married to Peter Waweru, who was previously married to a woman identified as Zipporah Njoki. The businessman and Njoki are divorced.

The sultry politician was accused of "stealing" another woman's husband, but she has denied the claims.

Speaking exclusively to TUKO.co.ke's Philip Momanyi, Waruguru said:

"I am not a husband snatcher. Mimi hukatiwa, sikatianangi (men chase me, I don't chase them). So please ask your men why they chase after me. After all, I'm not a husband snatcher, when you say you're snatched, it looks like you're an idle person who was walking around the street and you're pickpocketed."

She added:

"These women who are complaining need to check around their men and husbands and see which areas they can improve on."

Waruguru further revealed that he and Waweru are childhood friends, and he approached her first, and she accepted to date him.

"He is my man. Mkimuona mnamkubisha mama ni lion (If you see him, remind him that I am a lioness) so that he doesn't misbehave. But I will take good care of him," she said.

As earlier reported, Cate Waruguru’s husband, Peter Waweru, has rubbished claims that he tried to cheat on her with another female politician.

In an exclusive interview with TUKO.co.ke, Waweru also rubbished allegations that the woman rep had attacked his ex-wife, Zipporah Njoki.

"My wife’s foes are behind the false social media allegations. Some female politician from Mt Kenya region who tried to make advances at me is the one who began spreading the malicious allegations to taint our names," he said.

Waweru added the female politician, whom he did not name, initially wanted to have an affair with him, but he rejected her advances and made it clear that he is married to Waruguru.
