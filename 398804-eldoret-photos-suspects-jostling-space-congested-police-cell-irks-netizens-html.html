Eldoret: Photos of suspects piled up in police cell after violation of COVID-19 protocols spark angr

Publish date: 2024-03-02

- Photos taken from the inside of a police station showed suspects lumped together with disregard for social distancing

- Netizens wondered why the cops would arrest individuals for failing to observe social distancing then lump them together in close proximity

- The police service has time and again faced scrutiny over excessive use of force in enforcing the government's directives to stop the spread of COVID-19

- Records show that Kenya has recorded at least 15 deaths and over 30 injuries by police under the guise of pushing civilians to observe MOH guidelines

Kenyans have rallied together to condemn the apparent flaunting of COVID-19 protocols by police officers when handling suspects.

This was after a Facebook user shared photos of detainees lumped together at a packed police station in Eldoret.

Most of the suspects did not have masks.

The irony was that one of the reasons they were arrested was because they failed to observe the social distancing rule.

Netizens could not understand why police would break the law while trying to enforce the same law.

The Eldoret incident also raised concerns over the safety of detainees at police stations as the evident crowding provided fertile ground for the spread of COVID-19.

As has become the norm, fingers seemed to be pointing at corruption and extortion, a reputation that the police service has struggled to shrug off.

According to a section of Kenyans, the officers were not out to enforce any laws but rather they arrested the people to get bribes from them in exchange for freedom.

This once again put the Service on the spot regarding their approach to enforcing government directives.

In June 2020, three people were killed in the Rift Valley when policemen confronted and shot at residents for failing to wear face masks.

The latest case was that of Brian Nyaga, a 17-year-old Form Three student in Mombasa who was beaten by the cops when he tried to run away from being arrested for not having a mask on.

He is still nursing injuries and is yet to report back to school.

Kenya has so far recorded at least 15 deaths and over 30 injuries related to excessive use of force by the police since the COVID-19 restrictions came into the fore in early 2020.
