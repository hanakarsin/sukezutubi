Dark humour's meaning can vary from person to person. It is a comic style that makes light of subjects that are generally considered taboo. In certain instances, these subject matters are regarded as serious or painful to discuss. Some people might refer it to as jokes about life that are expressed in a distressing manner.

The perception of humour or something you find amusing is quite vague if you think about it. There isn't a formula written down defining something funny. Therefore, it is upon a person to make sense of what they consider funny. And this element makes humour one of the most complicated subjects.

Movies and TV shows perfectly depict dark humour. You have likely watched some of these productions and wondered how the characters joke about violence, war or disease. This is a classic example of dark humour, and you've likely laughed at such instances.

Common topics for dark humour

For dark humour to be effective, the subject has to be dark, of course. However, satire can provoke serious discussions about topics that people might not desire to talk about in certain instances. So, what are the common subjects that relate to dark humour?

  • War and terrorism.
  • Murder, violence, death.
  • Political corruption.
  • Human sexuality.
  • Racial or sexual stereotypes.
  • Poverty, disease, and famine.

The idea behind dark humour is to take the worst of humanity and make it funny. That is why a dark sense of humor is not for everyone, as such matters are often left undiscussed.

The history of black comedy

The most interesting thing is that the term black humour didn’t technically originate until 1935. During this time, Surrealist theorist Andre Breton was interpreting the works of Jonathan Swift, who is recognised for his essay, A Modest Proposal.

Is dark humor offensive?

The idea of dark comedy is controversial, especially considering its subjects are often disputed. But, today, the sense of humour is common with high profile comics on stage and social media platforms. It can bring people together while at the same time put them apart.

Although laughter has numerous physical benefits, there is a thin line between a harmless joke and what someone might consider an insensitive comment.

Therefore, dark humour can be offensive if it brushes someone the wrong way. After all, it creates comedy from topics that are ordinarily considered taboo to talk about. This way, people using dark humour ought to be careful not to sound blatantly disrespectful.

Dark sense of humor and intelligence

If you laugh at dark jokes, you are likely not evil but intelligent. This is according to a published study in the Cognitive Processing journal at the Medical University of Vienna. The study revealed a correlation between being smart and having a dark sense of humor.

According to the research, dark humour is:

Any kind of humour that treats sinister subjects like death, disease, deformity, handicap, or warfare with bitter amusement and presents such tragic, distressing, or morbid topics in humorous terms.

Does a dark sense of humor relate to dementia?

Research has shown that a dark sense of humor can be dementia warning sign. This is according to the University College of London that questioned families and friends of 48 dementia patients. The research concluded that someone’s sense of humour getting darker would be warning signs of dementia.

A dark sense of humour is excellent when used thoughtfully and creatively. The primary idea behind this kind of comedy is to bring people together that share similar traumas or dark experiences and help them heal. However, it is a great idea to use black comedy with caution.
