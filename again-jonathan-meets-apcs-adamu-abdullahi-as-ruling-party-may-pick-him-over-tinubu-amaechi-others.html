Again, Jonathan Meets APCs Adamu Abdullahi As Ruling Party May Pick Him Over Tinubu, Amaechi, Oth

Publish date: 2024-06-20

Ahead of the All Progressives Congress (APC) presidential primary, former President Goodluck Jonathan has again met with the ruling party’s national chairman, Senator Adamu Abdullahi.

Naija News reports that the meeting between the duo was held before the former president met with Mamman Daura, the influential nephew of the President Muhammadu Buhari.

The ruling APC has fixed its special National Convention to elect its presidential candidate for Sunday.

A source told The PUNCH that the meeting between Jonathan and Adamu, which took place at Jonathan’s Abuja home last week, lasted for about four hours.

The source said: “The photo of Mamman Daura and Jonathan was taken in Jonathan’s house. It was actually Mammand Daura that went to visit Jonathan. Also, the national chairman visited Jonathan twice last week. He met Jonathan late in the evening and they spoke till about 1am. The visit was for about four hours.

“Discussions are still ongoing and that is why the APC is willing to allow Jonathan to face the screening even though he didn’t submit his form ahead of the deadline.”

According to another source, Jonathan is not willing to contest for the APC presidential ticket against long-time members like Bola Tinubu, Ahmad Lawan, Yemi Osinbajo, Rotimi Amaechi and others.

He said the former president will only join the race if he is assured of emerging the consensus candidate of the party.

“Jonathan is a newcomer in the APC. He has no delegates of his own. You don’t expect him to be contesting openly against Bola Tinubu, Yemi Osinbajo and others. He will be setting himself up for defeat if he does that,” said the APC chieftain.
