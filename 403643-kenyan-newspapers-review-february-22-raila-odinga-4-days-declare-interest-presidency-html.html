Kenyan newspapers review for February 22: Raila Odinga has 4 days to declare his interest in preside

Publish date: 2024-05-22

On Monday, February 22, newspapers reported extensively on the 2022 succession politics continuing to gather steam and the push for constitutional amendments under the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI).

The dailies also covered disturbing reports about a student who died and at least two others gone blind after allegedly drinking hand sanitiser.

1. Daily Nation

Opposition leader Raila Odinga has until Friday, February 26, to declare whether or not he will vie for presidency come 2022.

The deadline was set by ODM's National Elections Board (NEB) weeks ago when it invited members who want to vie for the top seat on the party's ticket to express interest.

As of Sunday, February 21, Mombasa governor Hassan Joho was the only known ODM member who had notified the party's leadership about his presidential ambition and submitted his papers.

Raila, who has remained guarded on the matter, said he would only make a statement after a successful BBI referendum.

By Friday this week, more county assemblies are expected to have passed the proposed legislation, which has already been given the nod by nine county assemblies and rejected by one.

2. People Daily

Deputy President William Ruto has insisted that he remains unbowed from his quest to succeed President Uhuru Kenyatta when his term expires next year.

Ruto said to ensure that he is the next tenant at the state house, he has been crisscrossing the country asking for support from Kenyans while his competitors "sat in bars waiting to be endorsed".

According to the DP, he was aware of a plan to edge him and his allies from the government. He continued to accuse the Ethics and Anti Corruption Commission (EACC) and the police of being used by his enemies to settle political scores.

"I am working hard to succeed President Uhuru Kenyatta while some of my competitors are sitting in a bar somewhere waiting for endorsements. I have been moving across the country, initiating development projects, and asking for votes. Others have not even helped build a single charge even in their home areas," said Ruto.

3. Taifa Leo

The daily reports that the government has increased surveillance at the Namanga border after it emerged Tanzania was recording a surge in COVID-19 cases.

Rift Valley regional commissioner George Natembeya said the government was not taking any chance at the entry points and was working around the clock to ensure no infected people cross from either country.

This came as the WHO advised Tanzania to start reporting daily COVID-19 cases. The last time Tanzania reported the cases was in late April 2020, shortly after the disease struck the East Africa region.

President John Magufuli asked Tanzanians only to wear masks made they the country's Ministry of Health.

4. The Standard

The Standard reports that the latest moves made by Opposition leader Raila Odinga have left many guessing what the former premier is up to.

More confusing is his decision to stop attacking William Ruto's hustler narrative and even asking lawmakers seeking to criminalise the anecdote to drop the bid.

In what could be a pointer to a Raila candidature in 2022, the ODM chief told his NASA partners, Kalonzo Musyoka, Moses Wetang'ula and Musalia Mudavadi, not to expect his endorsement.

Raila has also held talks with the former United Nations Conference on Trade and Development (UNCTAD) secretary-general Mukhisa Kituyi who is eying presidency in 2022. Could Raila be seeking to endorse Kituyi? Or could the former Kimilili MP be joining Raila's fold?
