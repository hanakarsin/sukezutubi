Homa Bay county assembly joins Kisumu, Siaya in passing BBI bill

Publish date: 2024-07-17

- Homa Bay Members of the county assembly unanimously passed the BBI Bill 2020 just days after Kisumu did the same

- Siaya county passed the BBI draft bill on February 3, making it the first county assembly to vote in favour of the document

- This came days after Raila met thousands of delegates in Kisumu where he urged them to support the BBI

Homa Bay county assembly has become the third county to pass the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) Bill 2020 after Kisumu and Siaya assemblies.

The bill was tabled by the Justice and Legal Affairs committee chairperson Ajimba Ayieta on Thursday, February 11, and it was seconded by nominated ward rep Kevin Onyango

Reports indicated the Members of the county assembly unanimously passed the bill, once again, proving the overwhelming support the handshake support enjoys in ODM Raila Odinga's strongholds.

As earlier reported, Siaya county passed the BBI draft bill on February 3, making it the first county assembly to vote in favour of the document.

A couple of days ago, Kisumu took second place in the passing of the bill.

The report on the bill was tabled before the Assembly by Member of County Assembly (MCA)Seth Okumu, the Chairperson Administrative of Law and Justice Committee after the legislature resumed after a long recess.

Twenty-four out of the 47 counties, which form two-thirds representation have to vote in favour of the bill before it can be put through a referendum for the larger population of Kenyans to vote and either pass or reject it.

Raila meets delegates in Kisumu

This development came just days after Raila met thousands of delegates in Kisumu where he urged them to support the BBI.

Speakers at the event including, governor Anyang' Nyong'o', Woman Rep Rosa Buyu, nominated Senator Denita Ghati, as well as a host of MCAs, said they were fully behind BBI.

They declared it was compulsory for the region to back the document because of the fruits it promises.

The leaders who attended the meeting include all Luo Nyanza governors, several MPs, senators, woman reps and MCAs.
