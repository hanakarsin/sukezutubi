Jamhuri Day is marked as the day Kenya became a republic. Kenyans celebrate it every year since 1964 as it is a day that makes them feel proud about being Kenyan. Today, Kenyans send happy Jamhuri Day quotes and messages on social media to celebrate the progress the country has made and to celebrate fellow countrymen.

Jamhuri Day does not just end with text messages or social media posts, as you can pay your friends and family a visit. You can also attend the national event, which the president attends. Check out these touching, happy Jamhuri Day images with quotes you can share.

Happy Jamhuri Day wishes in 2023

What do you celebrate on Jamhuri Day? Being a free, independent nation is enough reason to celebrate. Citizens across the nation celebrate Kenya being a republic. The republic status has enabled citizens to freely move, live, and work in whichever corner of the country they wish to do so.

Tuko.co.ke published an article about Madaraka Day. Madaraka Day celebrations are held annually on 1st June, a national holiday to commemorate the day that Kenya achieved independent internal self-governance from the British. It is one of the three national holidays that have been created under Article 8 of the 2010 Constitution.

Kenyans have their unique way of celebrating this day. They gather together in thousands in a venue that has been selected for the event. The military organizes parades to showcase their talent.
