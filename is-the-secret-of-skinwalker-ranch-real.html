Is History Channel's 'The Secret of Skinwalker Ranch' Real?

Publish date: 2024-05-18

Is the History Channel Reality Series ‘The Secret of Skinwalker Ranch’ Real? You Be the Judge

By Bianca Piazza

Updated April 25 2023, 10:16 p.m. ET

Anyone familiar the History Channel's supernatural series knows of the ultra-meme-worthy show Ancient Aliens — which has been on since 2009. Well, the now-late creator of the said cult hit, Emmy winner Kevin Burns, brought us the paranormal series The Secret of Skinwalker Ranch alongside Joel Patterson in 2020.

UFO and “High Strangeness” phenomena is fruitful at the infamous Skinwalker Ranch, which is located in Utah's Uinta Basin. Astrophysicist Dr. Travis Taylor, real estate magnate Brandon Fugal (who owns the ranch), and their team of nerdy scientists set out to get to the bottom of a slew of strange and unexplainable happenings. All we have to say is jinkies!

Now on its fourth season — which premiered on April 18, 2023 — The Secret of Skinwalker Ranch highlights bizarre animal deaths (like mutilated cattle), close encounters, and seemingly convenient data-collecting interruptions that occur at the "world's most mysterious hotspot." Deemed "UFO alley" in the 1950s, the 512-acre Skinwalker Ranch may just be an alien polestar.

Especially considering the sci-fi-like reality series lives on the literal History Channel, many viewers (skeptics) want to know one thing: Is The Secret of Skinwalker Ranch real?

How real is the History Channel's 'The Secret of Skinwalker Ranch'?

Skeptics, die-hard alien believers, and tin foil hat–wearers alike, gather around. Let's get real for a second. Those who watch shows like the Travel Channel's Ghost Adventures, Animal Planet's Finding Bigfoot, or the History Channel's The Secret of Skinwalker Ranch don't typically dive into them assuming they'll see groundbreaking authentic footage of a real ghost, the legendary Sasquatch, or a green alien.

They're more about the journey, the history, the theories — honestly, these shows are downright campy. Finding Bigfoot lasted a whopping 12 seasons and even earned a Discovery Plus special in 2021 — Finding Bigfoot: The Search Continues — yet Bigfoot was never found.

While we can't deny or confirm that the otherworldly oddities that've plagued Skinwalker Ranch for decades are real, we will say that The Secret of Skinwalker Ranch is a nonfiction docuseries. Basically, it isn't a mockumentary.

Brandon Fugal never expected to witness anything supernatural when he purchased the property in 2016.

Not only was millionaire Brandon Fugal a skeptic when he bought Skinwalker Ranch, but he expected to reveal his identity.

"I acquired the property from billionaire Robert Bigelow for the purpose of conducting scientific research to determine if there was any validity to the extraordinary claims of paranormal activity," he explained to Newsweek in April 2022.

"Although I acquired the ranch as a skeptic, I eventually had my own undeniable Close Encounter — a UFO sighting in broad daylight with multiple witnesses." Everything changed after that.

"I never intended to reveal my identity as the owner, requiring strict confidentiality agreements and liability waivers. I kept my identity as the owner secret until being persuaded to go public in connection with the the History Channel docuseries and our ongoing investigation," Brandon continued.

When questioned about his true intentions for owning the notorious ranch, and whether or not he wished to profit off of its decades-long paranormal association, Brandon said, "I have yet to put a penny in my pocket personally regarding this endeavor."

Whether or not you're a believer, give The Secret of Skinwalker Ranch a shot; maybe it will change your mind.

Season 4 episodes of The Secret of Skinwalker Ranch air Tuesdays at 9 p.m. EST on the History Channel.
