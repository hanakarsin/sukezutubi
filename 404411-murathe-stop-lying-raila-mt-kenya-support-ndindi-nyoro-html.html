Murathe should stop lying to Raila he has Mt Kenya support, Ndindi Nyoro

Publish date: 2024-06-21

- Ndindi Nyoro asked Murathe to stop deceiving the ODM leader insisting the people of Central Kenya cannot and will never vote for him

- The lawmaker further questioned why Murathe had never accompanied the former premier to the region to sell his agenda to the people

Kiharu Member of Parliament Ndindi Nyoro has dismissed Jubilee Party vice-chairman David Murathe's remarks over ODM leader Raila Odinga's popularity in the Mount Kenya region.

Murathe, while attending a rally in Kajiado, said Central residents will consider the Opposition chief's presidential candidature in the 2022 General Election.

However, speaking in Gatanga on Sunday, February 28, Nyoro asked Murathe to stop deceiving the ODM leader insisting the people central cannot and will never vote for him.

"I have been watching you (Murathe) with Raila in Kajiado County. You've lied to him that the people of Murang'a can consider him for leadership. Let me be categorical, the people of Mount Kenya cannot even consider giving Raila Odinga their votes and should he come here, no one will bother attending his rally, we will all look the other way," said Nyoro.

The lawmaker further questioned why Murathe had never accompanied the former premier to the region to sell his agenda to the people.

He insisted that the region has never elected him to represent them or talk on their behalf.

We are too busy for BBI

The Tangatanga allied MP further said the faction does not have time to discuss the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI).

He said the hustler nation had bigger agendas for the country and was focused on developing and empowering the hustlers.

The Deputy President William Ruto's point man asked the BBI proponents to delink them from the politics of the report because they are not interested.

Kikuyus will never support Raila

As previously reported, Gatundu South MP Moses Kuria had also told President Uhuru Kenyatta that Kikuyus will never love or accept his handshake partner.

Speaking during a church service in the area on Sunday, February 28, Kuria said Kikuyus had already made up their minds that Ruto will be the next head of state after Uhuru finishes his second and final term.

"In as much as we respect you, we know that we elected you and not Raila. There is no day that we will ever love Raila," he said.
