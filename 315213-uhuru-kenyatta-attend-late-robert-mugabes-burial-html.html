Uhuru Kenyatta to attend late Robert Mugabes burial

Publish date: 2024-04-08

- Mugabe died while receiving treatment in Singapore on Friday, September 6

- Remains of the late former head of state was expected to leave Singapore on September 11

- The family and state are yet to agree on whether he be buried at his home

- 11 current and former heads of State have confirmed their attendance of his funeral service

President Uhuru Kenyatta is scheduled to travel to Harare, Zimbabwe for the burial of founding president and pan-Africanist Robert Gabriel Mugabe.

Uhuru is among other 11 current and former heads of state who have confirmed attendance of Mugabe's funeral event provisionally slated for Saturday, September 14, at Harare sports stadium.

The 95-year-old strongman passed on while undergoing treatment in Singapore on Friday, September 6, and his body flown to Zimbabwe on Wednesday, September 11.

Following the demise, Uhuru ordered all public institutions to fly the national flag half-mast in honour of the late leader.

He mourned him as Kenya's closest and a shining beacon of Africa’s liberation struggle.

However, until Thursday, September 12, the family and government were yet to agree on the burial site.

Whereas the State wanted Mugabe to be laid to rest at the cemetery for heroes of the liberation war at the Heroes Acre, next to his first wife Sally's grave, some family members want him interred at his village home in Zvimba district.
