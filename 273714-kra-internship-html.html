KRA Internship 2024/2025: careers, application and requirements

Publish date: 2024-07-16

The KRA Internship program is a government youth initiative to develop young talent for the country's labor market. This state agency offers continuing degree students a 3-month industrial attachment and a 12-month internship to fresh diploma and degree graduates.

KRA internship 2024/2025

KRA is a government revenue collection, accounting, and assessment agency. Its headquarters are in Times Tower on Haile Selassie Avenue in Nairobi. KRA has six departments, each headed by a commissioner. The departments are as follows:

  • Department of Corporate Support Services
  • Department of Investigations and Enforcement
  • Domestic Services Department for medium and small taxpayer
  • Custom Services Department
  • Department of Technical Support Services
  • Domestic Taxes Department for large taxpayers

KRA internship requirements

Here are the requirements for KRA internship opportunities:

  • Be of age 20-35 years and a Kenyan Citizen.
  • Have a first Diploma or Degree (undergraduate) from a recognized institution.
  • Be available for full-time work for twelve months.
  • Have never undertaken an internship or been exposed to work experience in your area of specialization after graduation.

KRA attachment requirements

Below are the requirements for the KRA attachment opportunities:

  • Be age 20-35 years and a Kenyan Citizen.
  • Be a continuing third or fourth-year student pursuing their first degree (undergraduate) student from a recognized university.
  • Have your university's valid introduction letter.
  • Be available to work full-time for three months.

KRA internship application process

KRA shares attachments and internship opportunities are shared in its e-recruitment portal. Only those registered on the portal can access the private KRA careers page and apply for available internships and attachments.

How to register on the KRA e-recruitment portal

Here is how to register on the KRA e-recruitment portal:

  • Fill in the required details on the registration page.
  • Accept the Data Privacy Statement.
  • Click Register.
  • KRA will send you an email requesting you confirm your email address.
  • Confirm your email address to complete your registration.
  • Refresh your email if you experience any delay in receiving an email notification at the end of the e-recruitment registration process, and in case of any challenge, send your query to kracareers@kra.go.ke.

How to apply for KRA internships or attachments

Now, register for the attachment/internship application using these simple steps:

  • Log into the KRA e-recruitment portal using the username and password you submitted when registering on the portal.
  • The KRA system will open the Applicant Cockpit.
  • To update or create your application profile (candidate profile), click Candidate Profile.
  • Click My Profile and follow the instructions to update or create your application profile.
  • Click Overview, then tap Release.
  • Click the check box on the page to finish updating or creating your application profile.
  • After that, click Employment Opportunities on the Applicant Cockpit page.
  • Click Start under Job Search to see available vacancies.
  • Click Job Posting to view the job's full details.
  • Click Apply at the top of the page and follow the instructions.
  • Fill in all mandatory fields.
  • Review your application before clicking Send Application Now to submit it.
  • After clicking Send Application Now, accept the Data Privacy Statement.

Note that:

  • The Kenya Revenue Authority takes in interns annually.
  • KRA offers quarterly attachment, i.e., January to March, April to May, June to July, August to September, and October to December.
  • KRA doesn't charge any fee for this process.
  • Incomplete applications will not be considered.
  • Only selected candidates are contacted.
  • The agency doesn't guarantee employment after the internship ends.
  • The Kenya Revenue Authority doesn't extend its internship and attachment programs.
  • KRA only absorbs interns and attaches permanently if vacancies are available.

Is the Kenya Revenue Authority a good employer?

KRA is among Kenya's top ten best-paying employers. Interns and attaches who eventually get long-term contracts at this government agency earn huge salaries.

Is KRA recruiting interns?

Check the KRA e-recruitment portal to know whether the KRA internship application deadline. The agency shares attachments and internship opportunities in the portal. Only those registered on the portal can access the private KRA careers page and apply for available internships and attachments.

Usually, KRA accepts applications for its 12-month internship towards the end of the year. For instance, applications for the January to December 2024 KRA internship program were accepted between August and November 2023.

Has KRA shortlisted candidates 2024?

Due to the large number of applications received, KRA does not publicly post the names of the shortlisted candidates for the internship program. Instead, only shortlisted candidates are contacted. If you are not contacted, you can try your luck next time.

How much are interns at KRA paid?

KRA pays interns a monthly stipend of Kshs. 15,000, which is subject to statutory deductions.
