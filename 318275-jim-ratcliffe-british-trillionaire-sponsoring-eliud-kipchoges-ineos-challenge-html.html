Jim Ratcliffe: British billionaire sponsoring Eliud Kipchoge's INEOS challenge

Publish date: 2024-03-08

- Jim Ratcliffe is the man behind Eliud Kipchoge's INEOS challenge set for Saturday, October 12

- The entrepreneur is ranked among one of the richest people in Britain with an estimated worth of over KSh 1.1 trillion

- Sir Jim started his INEOS company in 1992 and grew it to one of the biggest chemical firms

- The billionaire recently ventured into sports and has been sponsoring many other sport activities

When Eliud Kipchoge set to train for INEOS challenge four months ago, very few Kenyans knew about the man behind the race until the day the marathoner was flown in a private jet to Vienna, Austria.

The KSh 2.4 billion jet belonged to one of Britain's richest man, Sir James Arthur Ratcliffe, who owns Inspec Ethylene Oxide Specialities (INEOS), a firm that deals in chemicals.

The Financial Times newspaper describes Ratcliffe as a business enthusiast and great risk taker who grew one of Britain's biggest chemical firms from scratch.

Born in 1952, he grew up in a council house in Greater Manchester, attending grammar school before studying chemical engineering at the University of Birmingham.

The billionaire graduated from the university in 1974 and proceeded to London Business School where he graduated with an MBA in 1980.

Armed with the papers, he joined Esso before gaining business experience at a US private equity group.

In 1992, together with his friend John Hollowood, a former chemicals executive, they began looking for investment opportunities.

The duo led a buyout of a chemicals business to form Inspec, with Hallowood as chairman and Ratcliffe as chief executive.

However, the biggest break came in 1998 when Ratcliffe decided to put all his money into the business.

It took him a year to think about the deal before he mortgaged his house and put all the money in order to own the business fully.

“I was 40-years old. It is a very critical part of your career path...If it goes wrong you’ve lost all your money and completely screwed up your career," he said as quoted by a 2014 Financial Times article.

Armed with the money and combination of loans, he bought Antwerp site from which INEOS was born.

The company now has a turnover of KSh 5.4 trillion and sites across 26 countries.

His personal fortune - an estimated KSh 1.1 trillion - led him to reach the number one spot of the 2018 UK Sunday Times rich list.

In the recent times, he has come out as a sports enthusiast after he bought Belstaff, the luxury motorcycle clothing maker and became the sponsor of the British professional cycle team formerly called Team Sky.

He also funded Ben Ainslie's 2021 America's Cup sailing challenge and owns Swiss soccer club, Lausanne Sport.

It is his latest venture into sports that has seen him sponsor Eliud Kipchoge for the Vienna 1:59 challenge which is set for Saturday, October 12.
