Tanzanian President Samia Suluhu Reshuffles Cabinet Days after Being Sworn in

Publish date: 2024-06-03

- President Samia Suluhu Hassan appointed Liberata Mulamula as the new Minister for Foreign Affairs and International Cooperation

- She replaced Professor Palamagamba Kabudi, who was moved to the Ministry of Justice and Constitutional Affairs

- Kabudi took over from Mwigulu Nchemba, who will now be the Minister of Finance and Planning

- The docket was left vacant following the appointment of Philip Mpango as the country's vice president

President Samia Suluhu Hassan has appointed veteran diplomat Liberata Mulamula to succeed Professor Palamagamba Kabudi as Minister of Foreign Affairs and International Cooperation.

Before her new appointment, Mulamula was the ministry's permanent secretary and also worked as the first executive secretary of the International Conference on the Great Lakes Region (ICGLR).

The Citizen reported that at some point, she served as Tanzania's ambassador to the United States (US).

Hussein Matanga, Tanzania's ambassador to Japan, has been appointed as her new chief secretary.

He succeeds Bashiru Ally, who will be one of her nominated members of parliament.

Ally, the former General Secretary of the CCM, had only been in the role for one month.

The soft-spoken former vice president was sworn-in on Friday, March 19, as Tanzania's first female president after the sudden death of John Magufuli from an illness shrouded in mystery.
