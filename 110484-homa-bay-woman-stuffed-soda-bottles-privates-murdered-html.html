Police investigate grisly murder of woman in Homa Bay

Publish date: 2024-07-03

- A woman's body was found in Homa Bay's Wire Forest wrapped in a polythene bag, and two bottles of soda next to it, believed to have been used to sexually harras her

- Police said the woman's husband often accused her of cheating on him, and the two fought frequently over the accusations

Police in Homa Bay are investigating the gruesome murder of a 30-year-old woman whose body was found wrapped in a paper bag and dumped in Wire Forest in Rachuonyo North Sub-County, Homa Bay County.

Area police were informed of the issue after pupils of Nyakwere Primary School spotted the body, identified as that of Jackline Akoth Ouma, as they were headed to school early Monday morning on March 21.

Homa Bay police commander John Omusanga who confirmed the incident said the body was found with deep cuts and that two soda bottles were found adjacent to it, an indication that her assailant(s) may have used them to sexually abuse her.

The woman’s husband Calvince Ouma Owuor, who had reportedly been threatening to kill her over alleged infidelity, was arrested to assist with investigations.

According to a KNA report, the police boss said the deceased had frequent fights with her husband, as he accused her of cheating on him.

Ouma will be charged in court upon completion of investigations

The body was moved to Rachuonyo Level Four Hospital mortuary where a post mortem examination will be done.
