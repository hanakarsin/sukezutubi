Kenyan wife of missing Dutch billionaire to face murder charges

Publish date: 2024-04-14

The prosecution on Thursday preferred murder charges against Sarah Wairimu, the wife of missing Dutch national Tob Cohen, following the mysterious disappearance of her husband.

Wairimu, however, did not take plea as the court directed she undergoes mental assessment before answering to the charges.

Wairimu is alleged to have killed Cohen in July 2019 within Nairobi county acting jointly with others not before the court, according to Citizen TV.

The defence team led by lawyer Philip Murgor strongly opposed the preferred charges saying the body of the deceased was non-existent.

“Before there’s an application for mental assessment the documents must be complete,” said Murgor.

“We want the evidence that supports the charge,” Murgor said.

State prosecutor Catherine Mwaniki, however, said there are witnesses who need to be protected, adding that the prosecution was satisfied it had sufficient reasons to prefer murder charges against Wairimu.

Mwaniki also submitted that murder charges can be preferred even in the absence of the body.

She argued the accused has to undergo mental checkup before answering to the murder charge.

The matter will be mentioned on Monday, September 16.

Tob Cohen is reported to have gone missing in July 2019.

The tours and travel magnate had lived in Kenya for 32 years.

He and Wairimu were embroiled in a bitter divorce case which saw him file assault charges against her a week before he went missing.
