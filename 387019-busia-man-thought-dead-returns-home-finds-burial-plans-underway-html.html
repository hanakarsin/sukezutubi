Busia: Man thought to be dead returns home, finds his burial plans underway

Publish date: 2024-07-29

- William Ong'ono's family thought he died in a boat accident in Lake Victoria and kickstarted his burial plans

- However, he returned home and found out his family presumed he was dead because another man, who had the same name as his, perished in the accident

- The 55-year-old will undergo some cleansing rituals so that he can fully be welcomed back to the community

A man who was presumed to be dead has returned home only to find his burial plans taking place.

William Ong'ono was believed to have died a few days ago in a boat accident in Lake Victoria and his family had commenced his funeral plans even before confirming whether he had indeed passed away.

According to a report by NTV, in the material day of the accident, Ong'ono wanted to use the ill-fated boat but since it was filled with passengers, he instinctively decided to wait for another one and that, thankfully, is the reason he is still alive.

"Everybody knows me as Mkulima and so in the boat, there was another rider who was also called Mkulima and so when the boat accident occurred, they thought it was me," Ong'ono said.

Ong'ono's cleansing rituals

Since his burial plans had commenced when he came home, the 55-year-old man will have to undergo a series of cleansing rituals to allow him back home.

He has been in a makeshift shelter for three days after a ceremony in which a rooster was killed to allow him to enter his father's house. Another will also be slaughtered to allow him live.

Being a bachelor, his sister-in-law has been staying with him in the shelter for the past three days, assuming the place of his would-be wife.

"We have been with him here for three days, once four days are over, we will go with him to his house," Ruth Obuya, the sister-in-law said.

So elaborate are the cleansing plans that a house will have to be built for Ong'ono where he will now start his new life and be fully accepted back to the community.

In a separate story, Former Webuye MP Musikari Kombo's brother was buried on Saturday, October 17, in a ceremony where some unique cultural practices were witnessed.

Being from the Balanda clan of Bukusu sub-tribe in Bungoma county, the deceased was laid to rest while in a sitting position, a burial practice the elders said has been on for years.

Enock Sirongo, chairman of the Balanda clan said the peculiar cultural practice was adopted after their leader, Mulunda, died while seated on an anthill.
