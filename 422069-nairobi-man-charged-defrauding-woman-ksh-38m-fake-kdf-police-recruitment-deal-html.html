Nairobi Man Charged with Defrauding Woman KSh 3.8m in Fake KDF, Police Recruitment Deal

Publish date: 2024-02-08
  • Johnstone Mutua obtained KSh 3.8 million from Rose Adhiambo by pretending he was in a position to recruit her relatives into KDF
  • Having relatives that were in dire need of jobs, the lady gave the accused the money, but the deal never materialised
  • Appearing before Milimani Chief Magistrate Francis Andayi, Mutua pleaded not guilty

A Nairobi man has been arraigned for allegedly obtaining KSh 3.8 million from a lady by false pretence.

Johnstone Mutua was accused of receiving the cash from Rose Adhiambo by pretending he could recruit her relatives into the Police Service and Kenya Defense Forces (KDF).

The court was told that Mutua approached Adhiambo in January 2021 when the KDF recruitment exercise was announced and told her he had someone in authority that could help him get some slots.

Later, Mutua, a matatu businessman, told the complainant (Adhiambo) that he had secured three KDF slots trading at KSh 300,000 each, K24 reported.

Having three relatives in dire need of jobs, the lady gave the accused a total of KSh 900,000, topping it up with other cash to cater for the logistics.

Further, the businessman told her that he had another two slots in the Police Service that were going for KSh 450,000 and she fell for it.

Appearing before Milimani Chief Magistrate Francis Andayi, Mutua pleaded not guilty and was released on KSh 300,000 cash bail.

Five military officers charged

Elsewhere, five military officers serving in the KDF were arraigned before the court-martial at Kahawa Barracks to answer various charges of recruiting malpractices.

The five officers were accused of receiving money from civilians with the promise of influencing their recruitment and successful listing into the force.

Unlike in the civil court, where the accused persons faced trial while standing in the dock, the accused officers sat before the bench comprising the advocate judge and court members.

In related news, a former KDF officer was convicted by the Isiolo Barracks court-martial to 42 months in prison after he was found guilty of soliciting and obtaining bribes by pretence.

This is after the accused lied to his two victims who are both Kenyan citizens that he would influence their enrollment into the military.
