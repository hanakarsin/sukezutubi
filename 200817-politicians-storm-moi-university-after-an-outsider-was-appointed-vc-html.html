Politicians oppose appointment of 'outsider' as Moi University VC

Publish date: 2024-03-19

- Politicians have stormed Moi University after Education CS Fred Matiang'i appointed Laban Ayiro as the acting VC

- The politicians want their tribesman to be given the post

Professor Richard Mibey has been at the helm as Vice Chancellor of Moi University for the last decade and his term came to an end recently.

He went on a compulsory leave which would pave way for his retirement but returned in an unclear circumstances.

However, Mibey finally left and his place was given to Laban Ayiro as the acting VC for the next three months as the institution looks to get his lasting replacement.

However, that has not gone down well with a section of leaders from the Kalenjin as they have opposed the move.

For starters, Moi University is based in Eldoret, the heart of Kalenjinland.

Led by Governors Jackson Mandago (Uasin Gishu county) and his Elgeyo Marakwet counterpart Alex Tolgos, the leaders stormed the institution Tuesday, September 20, to demand that Education CS Fred Matiang’I revokes the appointment of Ayiro.

The angry leaders demanded that instead their own tribesman, Isaac Kosgey, be appointed the VC of the institution.

According to them, Kosgey topped the interviews but they don’t understand how he was not picked.

The leaders have now threatened to block the graduation ceremony slated for Friday, September 24, 2016.

The leaders said they will block roads by cutting trees and laying them on the roads.

They claimed the appointment was made when DP William Ruto is out of the country.

Others who stormed the institution included MPS Oscar Sudi (Kapseret), James Bett (Kesses) and Sila Tiren (Moiben).
