Bishop Wanjiru Says Kenya Is Blessed to Have Deputy President Who Can Quote Bible

Publish date: 2024-04-27
  • Bishop Margaret Wanjiru asked Kenyans to continue praying for Deputy President William Ruto as he seeks to lead Kenya as her fifth president
  • Wanjiru said Kenya was blessed due to Ruto's unmatched knowledge of the scriptures
  • The Jesus Is Alive Ministries founder also seeks to be Nairobi's governor in the next year's general vote

Former Starehe MP Margaret Wanjiru has hailed Deputy President William Ruto for his mastery of the Bible.

Speaking in a Nairobi church on Sunday, July 11, Wanjiru said Kenya is blessed to have such a leader who founds each of his undertakings on the Bible.

She called on Kenyans to pray for Ruto as he seeks to succeed President Uhuru Kenyatta in the next year's polls.

"We are blessed to have a deputy president that can quote the scriptures. God-willing, and God helping us, we will be swearing him in as our next president by next year. So let us persist in prayers and support him where we can," said Wanjiru amid cheers from the faithful.

"I thank God for the great anointing God has given me. We are going to take over Nairobi as the governor come next year," she said.

Wanjiru pledges support for Ruto

As previously reported, Wanjiru had said Ruto is best suited for the nation's top seat as he doesn't have hangovers like his fellow leaders who always drink.

Speaking in a church service at PCEA Umoja, Embakasi West, on Sunday, July 4, Wanjiru said that this time around, Nairobi and Central Kenya would fully back the deputy president in 2022.

''I will support William Ruto because he does not have hangovers, unlike some leaders we see around. He is sober and thats why he always visits different churches to offer his prayers to the almighty father in heaven,'' said Wanjiru.

Wanjiru also took a swipe at President Uhuru Kenyatta on his move to form a coalition agreement with the former prime minister Raila Amollo Odinga ahead of the 2022 polls.

"It is his democratic right to support whoever he wants to support but Kenyans will decide who will be president, citizens will vote for the leaders they want. For us, we know who is our presidential candidate, William Ruto, who we will go all out to ensure that he wins elections," she added.
