Fire Guts Down Dormitory at Ringa Boys High School in Homa Bay

Publish date: 2024-05-18
  • The dormitory which used to accommodate approximately 400 Form Four students started burning at dawn
  • School principal Austine Muma Okello said the inferno started when the students had already gone for their morning studies
  • The principal further appealed to well-wishers to cheap in so that they help the students stay at school

Learning activities at Ringa Boys High school in Kabondo Kasipul constituency within Homa Bay county were disrupted today after their dormitory was set ablaze.

The dormitory which used to accommodate approximately 400 Form Four students started burning early this morning.

According to the school principal Austine Muma Okello, the inferno started when the students had already gone for their morning preps.

‘‘The fire started when the students had already gone to their various classes and the cause of the fire is not yet determined," said Muma.

He informed the press that they had already held a meeting with the county and sub-county directors of education and other government ministries over the incident.

The principal further appealed to well-wishers to cheap in so that they help the students stay at school.

Isaiah Juma Ondoro who is the area assistant chief requested the members of the public to inform them of any suspects.

‘‘The authorities have begun their investigations and we will disclose to you the outcome once they are done,’’ said Ondoro.

Kojwach ward MCA Joan Ogada called on the security officials to tighten security around the area to ensure safety.

She also appealed to the well-wishers to help the students since their exams are around the corner.

Fire incidences

The latest development came two days after such cases were reported in Nairobi's Ofafa Jericho High School and Sigalame Boys' in Busia county.

Sigalame recorded the fourth inferno in a span of three months on Monday, September 20.

"It is not normal for a single school to be burnt down at this rate. Students are calm and we suspect outside forces," school principal Peter Auma lamented.

In Ofafa Jericho, firefighters in the Kamukunji sub-county managed to put out the fire.

The police established that a student who was protesting against evening preps set one of the dormitories on fire.

While mobilising students to attend evening preps, the school principal, Julius Linge, said some defied and remained within the dormitory.

This prompted the teacher on duty to force the learners out, but one of the students sneaked back into the dormitory and torched one cubicle housing 20 students.

Story by Zabbibah Adhiambo, Homa Bay correspondent.
