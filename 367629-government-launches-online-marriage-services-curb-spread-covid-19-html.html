Government launches online marriage services to curb spread of COVID-19

Publish date: 2024-04-19

- Couples seeking to tie the knot at Sheria House will have to seek marriage registration services online after the Office of the Attorney General unveiled a digital platform

- The new e-registration platform will enable both Kenyan and foreign nationals to initiate and complete the process of registration of marriage services online

-Couples will only visit the registrar’s office on the final day after all other processes have been concluded online

- On the marriage day, the couple will only be accompanied by two witnesses and the registrar will officiate the ceremony within 20 minutes

- The services which are currently limited to Nairobi residents will be decentralized to other regions in August

Couples who intend to get married will have to do the entire registration process on the government's eCitizen portal unless otherwise.

The government launched the online marriage services in a bid to eliminate human traffic at Sheria House and curb the spread of the coronavirus outbreak.

State Law Office Chief Administrative Secretary Winnie Guchu said couples should not visit the registrar of marriages at Sheria House unless it is of utmost importance.

“There will be no more manual services at the registrar of marriage at Sheria House as all services will be available online. We do not want couplea coming at Sheria House," she said.

Couples planning to do a civil wedding will now be required to register their details on the eCitizen portal.

They will then pay for the marriage fee on the same platform, book for a pre-wedding interview and later get an appointment for the wedding to be officiated by the registrar within 20 minutes.

The schedule will have to be adhered to and if not, the couples will have to book new dates ad on the marriage day the couple will only be accompanied by two witnesses only.

“After completion of registration on the platform, couples will still be given 21-days’ notice for approval just as it was in the manual process," said Gachu.

"However, this process will be first rolled out in Nairobi, and thereafter in other counties after the platform takes root,” the CAS added.

An influx of couples seeking marriage services at the government office had brought a dilemma on enforcing COVID -19 measures forcing it to suspend them.

However, when the services briefly resumed in mid-May, 1,841 couples, whose marriages were suspended wanted to proceed with their civil wedding.

According to Gachu, the move brought enormous challenges with only 700 couples Willing to postpone their wedding.

“When we resumed, only 700 couples were willing to postpone their weddings however we were left with a bulk of 1,841 couples," she said."We are hoping that more people will postpone their weddings so we can manage the numbers,” she added.
