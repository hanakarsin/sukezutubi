Kibra decides: McDonald Mariga chased from polling station, one arrested

Publish date: 2024-03-27

- Mariga and supporters visited Ayany polling station to inspect the ongoing Kibra by-election

- His presence at the centre caught attention of several youths who kept watch

- The youths demanded he leaves the station immediately prompting police to open fire

Police were forced to shoot in the air to disperse marauding youths who were itching to attack Jubilee Party candidate McDonald Mariga at Ayany polling station in Kibra during voting.

Drama started at about 4pm when Mariga drove into the centre in a convoy of about five cars but his presence ignited an open revolt from hordes of opposing voters.

A number of Jubilee politicians including the former Kenyan international were inspecting the ongoing exercise which started on the morning of Thursday, November 7.

The situation turned ugly as youths shouted kwenda! kwenda! (go away, go away) while others aimed at his sleek car with stones, prompting security men to open fire.

They demanded he leaves the centre immediately fearing he could engage in an electoral malpractice.

Mariga had to show a clean pair of heels to save his dear life from the red eyed rowdy youths.

One person was arrested for allegedly stoning the politician's car.

Pockets of violence were witnessed at most stations even as the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) urged agents and voters to desist from any breach of law.

Kimilili MP Didmus Barasa and his Kapseret counterpart Oscar Sudi were caught up in the melee.

Former Kakamega senator Boni Khalwale was not spared either as he came into close contact with stone-throwing goons.

The voting exercise came to a close at 5pm.
