Insecurity: Tinubu Regrets Nigerians' Frustration, Anger

Publish date: 2024-04-05

A chieftain of the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP), Daniel Bwala, has said President Bola Tinubu regrets the anger and frustration expressed by Nigerians over the rising insecurity in the country.

Bwala, who served as a campaign spokesperson to the PDP candidate in the 2023 presidential election, Atiku Abubakar, stated this in a post via his official X handle on Wednesday.

The PDP chieftain assured Nigerians that President Tinubu would do everything possible to address the security and economic challenges confronting the country.

Bwala said the hydra-headed security challenge is a test to Nigerians, but the president would ensure it’s resolved in due time.

He, therefore, charged the citizens to remain resilient and keep faith in the country, stressing that Nigeria is a unique country of resilient people.

He wrote: “Fellow countrymen, keep faith with your country; Nigeria is a unique country of resilient people.

“@officialABAT will do whatever he can to address the myriad of challenges confronting the Nigerian people.

“Your frustration and anger are deeply regretted. But I assure you that the labour of our heroes past shall never be in vain.

“The hydra-headed security challenge is testing our resolve, but If we stay hopeful and resilient amidst these trying times, we will rise up undaunted as a nation.”
